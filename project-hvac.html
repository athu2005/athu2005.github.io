HVAC Coil Optimization for Efficiency

Iterative MATLAB Simulation with Quasi-ML Approach for Thermal Performance

Overview

This self-initiated research project focused on optimizing HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning) coil efficiency through computational modeling and quasi-machine learning approaches. The goal was to identify optimal design parameters that maximize thermal performance while considering practical manufacturing constraints.

Problem Statement

HVAC coil design involves complex tradeoffs:

  • Increasing coil diameter improves heat transfer but increases material cost and space requirements
  • Modifying longitudinal and transverse spacing affects both thermal efficiency and pressure drop
  • Air mass flow rate needs optimization to balance comfort, energy consumption, and noise levels
  • Environmental variables (humidity, temperature, seasonal variations) impact real-world performance

Methodology

Core Approach: Developed a parametric MATLAB simulation model connecting coil design parameters to thermal output

  • Simulation Model: Iterative numerical solver for heat transfer differential equations
  • Parameter Space: Coil diameter, longitudinal spacing, transverse spacing, air mass flow rate
  • Quasi-ML Technique: Used ISEER (Integrated Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio) evaluations as performance metric
  • Environmental Integration: Incorporated humidity variations, thermal loads across seasons, and climate-specific data
  • Optimization: Iteratively tested parameter combinations to identify Pareto-optimal designs

Key Design Parameters Optimized

Coil Diameter: Balanced between heat transfer surface area and spatial constraints

Tube Spacing: Optimized both longitudinal (flow direction) and transverse (perpendicular to flow) spacing

Air Flow Rate: Tuned for efficiency without excessive noise or comfort issues

Material & Fin Configuration: Analyzed different fin geometries for maximum heat transfer coefficient

Environmental Integration

The model incorporated real-world environmental variables that traditional coil design often overlooks:

  • Seasonal humidity variations affecting latent cooling requirements
  • Geographic climate patterns for different Indian regions
  • Dynamic thermal loads based on building occupancy and usage patterns

Results & Optimization Outcomes

The quasi-ML approach using ISEER evaluations identified optimal coil configurations that deliver:

  • 15-20% improvement in seasonal energy efficiency compared to baseline designs
  • Reduced pressure drop maintaining comfort levels
  • Feasible manufacturing within current industry standards
  • Adaptability to different climate zones and usage patterns

MATLAB Implementation Highlights

Solver: Finite difference scheme with adaptive mesh refinement

Visualization: 3D parameter space plots showing efficiency landscape

Validation: Compared simulated results with published experimental data

Extensibility: Modular code structure for easy incorporation of new correlations or environmental models

Practical Applications

  • Design recommendations for HVAC manufacturers targeting Indian markets
  • Framework for retrofitting existing systems with optimized coils
  • Energy consumption predictions for building certification (LEED, IGBC compatibility)
  • Foundation for IoT-enabled predictive maintenance systems
