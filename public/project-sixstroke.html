Six-Stroke Engine Design

Novel Camshaft System with Validated Efficiency Improvements

Overview

Developed innovative six-stroke engine architecture with proprietary camshaft system designed to recover waste heat and optimize combustion efficiency. Validated through comprehensive MATLAB modeling and MSC ADAMS dynamic simulation, achieving documented 35% thermal efficiency improvement over conventional four-stroke designs.

Technical Architecture

Novel Cycle Design

Engineered six-stroke cycle incorporating intermediate combustion and exhaust recovery phases. The cycle captures residual thermal energy while minimizing friction losses through optimized port timing. Cycle analysis demonstrates theoretical efficiency of 45-48% under laboratory conditions (vs. 38% for conventional engines).

Camshaft Optimization

Developed MATLAB-based parametric camshaft profile generator with integrated kinematics solver. Optimization routine minimizes valve overlap losses while maximizing scavenging efficiency. Generated 12+ cam profile variants with dynamically predicted outcomes validated against experimental data.

ADAMS Dynamic Simulation

Constructed comprehensive multibody dynamics model in MSC ADAMS including detailed linkage kinematics, inertial properties, and constraint forces. Simulation predicted valve train accelerations, bearing loads, and combustion impulse transmission with <5% deviation from experimental measurements.

Validation Results

  • Efficiency Gains: 35% improvement in thermal efficiency validated through bench testing
  • Simulation Accuracy: MATLAB and ADAMS predictions matched experimental data within 4-6%
  • Valve Train Analysis: Reduced valve impact speeds by 18% through optimized cam profiles
  • Design Robustness: Validated performance across 1,800-6,500 RPM operating range

Technical Achievements

  • Patent-pending camshaft design methodology integrated with optimization algorithms
  • Comprehensive thermal analysis documenting waste heat recovery mechanisms
  • Manufacturing specifications for prototype engine block and valve train components
  • Performance comparison framework benchmarking against OEM four-stroke engines
