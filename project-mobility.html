Connected Mobility Testbed

Adapted Cyber-Physical Mobility Laboratory for Indian Traffic Conditions

Overview

This research project adapted IIT Bombay's Cyber-Physical Mobility Lab to study autonomous vehicle behavior under Indian traffic conditions. Working under Prof. Archak Mittal, I updated the complete vehicle dynamics model and hardware systems for a scaled 1:14 RC car platform.

Key Contributions

  • Hardware Adaptation: Modified MJX Hyper Go RC car with custom PCBs, power electronics, and 3D-printed sensor mounts for scale change (1:18 → 1:14)
  • ROS2 Implementation: Updated all ROS codes and control algorithms to account for new vehicle specifications and sensor configurations
  • Vehicle Dynamics Model: Replaced kinematic bicycle model with Ackermann steering model for improved accuracy in steering control
  • Motion Planning: Developed algorithms to minimize lateral tire slip and improve trajectory tracking in tight maneuvers
  • Field Testing: Conducted extensive testing in diverse traffic scenarios including congested urban roads and highway conditions

Technical Details

Vehicle Platform: MJX Hyper Go 1:14 RC Car with custom electronics and sensor suite

Software Stack: ROS2, Python, C++

Key Sensors: LiDAR, IMU, GNSS, Camera

Control System: Ackermann steering model with trajectory tracking algorithms

Impact & Results

The updated testbed successfully demonstrated improved simulation-to-reality transfer by minimizing lateral tire slip prediction errors. The vehicle dynamics model now accurately represents Indian traffic behavior including aggressive lane changes and congested driving patterns.

Challenges Overcome

  • Scaling vehicle dynamics equations for different RC car scales
  • Tuning control parameters for Indian traffic characteristics
  • Integrating custom hardware with existing ROS2 architecture
  • Minimizing computational latency for real-time control

Learning Outcomes

This project deepened my understanding of vehicle dynamics, control systems, and ROS2 implementation. I learned how to adapt research platforms for real-world conditions and the importance of iterative testing and validation.
